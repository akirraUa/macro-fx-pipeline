Macro FX
Pipeline.

An institutional analysis framework in three sequential levels. No level concludes alone — each one confirms, contradicts, or reframes the previous. Built to answer the question retail analysis skips: not what the trade is, but how the macro transmits into price and what specifically breaks the thesis.

03The mandate-gauge asymmetry

The idea that separates this from "ECB dovish, Fed hawkish → short EUR". The same shock transmits differently because each bank targets a different inflation gauge. Toggle the shock.

Fed targets core PCE

Core strips energy → oil barely moves the reaction function → stays on hold.

ECB targets headline HICP

Headline includes energy → oil feeds straight in → forced to hike into weak growth.
NET → divergence NARROWS as the ECB catches up hawkishly

Counterintuitive payoff: a structural USD-bullish / EUR-bearish thesis can be stronger in the oil-collapse case than in the spike — purely from which gauge each bank is bound to.

Three sequential levels → a thesis, a structural invalidation (a data event, never a price level), and a tactical note on near-term squeezes.
